Use the Internet safely:
Cybersecurity Essentials

"Four essentials to protect yourself and your computer."
01.

Install a Firewall

A firewall is software or hardware that builds a defensive wall around your computer. It checks data coming from the Internet and then either turns it away or allows it to pass, depending on your firewall settings.

In this way, it helps prevent hackers and malicious software from accessing your computer.

Why it matters

Without a firewall, your computer is directly exposed to every probe and attack from the open internet.

02.

Keep Software Up to Date

Most malware attacks are made by criminal gangs who have found ways of infiltrating known security weak spots. It’s important, therefore, to install the regular security updates offered by software for operating systems such as Microsoft Windows and plug-ins like Java.

Malware attacks now tend to come via the web browser, so it’s essential to have the latest version of your browser installed.

03.

Use Protection Software

Anti-virus software scans emails and other files on your computer for viruses, worms, and trojans. Anti-spyware programs alert you when spyware tries to install itself.

"
Because new malware is identified every day, protection software should always be allowed to run its automatic update when needed.
"
04.

Take Special Care with Email

Be careful when opening email attachments. Files attached to email messages are a primary source of virus infection. Never open an attachment from someone you don’t know.

Take care also when clicking hyperlinks in email messages. Hyperlinks are often used as part of phishing and spyware operations. Only click links in email messages that you trust.

Email Safety Tips

Consider installing a spam filter to prevent spam emails from reaching you. Never respond to a spam email, even to say no or ‘unsubscribe’; your reply is a confirmation of your email address.
